Copyright and Privacy

Research and discuss how copyright and privacy affect you as a web developer (take into account both inside and outside of New Zealand)
Hint: have a look into the Privacy Act (2003), Copyright Act (1994) and New Technologies Ammendements (2001)

Copyright

"The moment a work is created in a tangible form, it immediately becomes the property of the author who created it." - (Kyrnin, 2013)

Copyright is a way of protecting a persons intellectual property, be that an image, an article, music or art, and without express consent from the creator, the property cannot legally be used. This is a hugely important concept in web development as developers will constantly be using other peoples property to make thier websites and they need to ensure they are adhering by the copyright laws both locally (New Zealand) and in other countries.

Creatrive commons is a set of licenses that are used to give permisions with a piece of property, with each license allowing different things (Stieben, 2014). These licenses are all combinations of 4 main points:

  • Attribution - Must give credit to original creator
  • ShareAlike - If you can use thier content, they can use yours
  • Non-Commercial - Cannot be used to make a profit
  • NoDerivitaves - Content cannot be changed in anyway, has to be exactly the same as the original
Copyright is also a global issue and New Zealand has many international agreements to ensure this is maintained accorss countries, such as the Berne Convention, The Agrreement on Trade-Related Aspects of International Property Rights (TRIPS) and The Universal Copyright Convention 1952 (Ministry of Business)

Privacy

According to the Privacy Act (2003), any personal information collected within New Zealand that identifies an individual, such as names, phone numbers or emails etc must be protected and kept private. This is important in web development as they need to be very careful when using or collecting this type of data from users, else they can be liable for any losses a user has by being identified by the data.

Globally, there are over 80 countries with thier own privacy laws but most of them follow the same route as the New Zealand one, which helps to reeduce the confusion when dealing with international visitors, for example, which law would be followed if a American visited a New Zealand website that had its data stored in England? Luckily these laws are all very simillar and are designed to ensure the safe handling of user data, and to make sure no identifying data is displayed anywhere without consent.
